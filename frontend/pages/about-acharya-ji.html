Biography

Acharya Upendra Ji is a Jeevan Mukt Sadguru — a self-realized master who has attained liberation while living in the body — and the founder of Antar Yog Foundation. From an early age, he felt a deep calling toward the timeless wisdom of India's ancient scriptures, embarking on a rigorous inner journey guided by the revered Guru–Shishya tradition.

Through years of intense Sadhana and immersion in the Upanishads, Vedas, Bhagavad Gita, and classical non-dual texts, he arrived at the state of Jeevan Mukti — not as a distant ideal, but as a lived, embodied reality. He recognized that this liberation is available to every human being, not only to renunciants, but to householders, professionals, and seekers living ordinary lives.

Driven by the ancient principle of जनहिताय स्वयं मोक्षाय च — welfare of all and liberation of self — Acharya Ji founded Antar Yog Foundation to make the transformative power of Advaita Vedanta accessible to every stratum of society. Through Shibirs (immersive spiritual camps), Discourses, the residential Gurukul, and Nirjanwas retreats, he has personally guided over 10,000 seekers across 50+ cities.

Acharya Ji is deeply committed to the awakening of women, honoring the Durga Shakti — divine feminine power — within every person. He teaches that true national transformation begins when both men and women rise as compassionate, self-reliant leaders grounded in inner wisdom.

Beyond Vedanta, he revives classical Indian knowledge systems including Naadi Jyotish (ancient predictive astrology encoded on palm leaves), Vastu Rupantaran (the science of harmonizing living spaces with cosmic energy), and the healing traditions preserved in the scriptures. He teaches these not as rituals, but as living sciences rooted in universal principles.

His ultimate vision is a transformed Bharat — a nation of awakened, prosperous, and spiritually grounded citizens — that once again rises as a Vishwa Guru, a guiding light for all of humanity. Every program, every teaching, every Shibir is one step toward this great aspiration.
