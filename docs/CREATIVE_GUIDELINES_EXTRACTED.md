# Antar Yog Creative Guidelines - Web Design Reference

**Source:** Antar_Yog_Creative_Guidelines.pdf  
**Scope:** Brand identity, visual language, and design principles for all web creatives  
**Date Extracted:** April 2026

---

## 1. Brand Foundations

Antar Yog should feel:
- **Devotional** — spiritually grounded and reverent
- **Credible** — organized, authoritative, trustworthy  
- **Transformational** — aspirational, hopeful, outcomes-focused

Visuals can be emotional and festival-led, but must remain **clean, organized, and premium**.

### Three Consistent Visual Anchors
These must remain steady across all creatives, even when theme or festival changes:

1. **Antar Yog identity mark** — the logo
2. **Acharya imagery** — portraits and representations of the teacher
3. **Strong bottom CTA zone** — prominent call-to-action placement at footer/bottom

---

## 2. Logo System

### Mandatory Requirement
Every post/page needs a logo. This is **non-negotiable** and must be treated as a mandatory brand element across all creatives.

### Approved Lockups
- Use only the approved Antar Yog logo lockups
- Available in English and Devanagari versions
- Symbol + wordmark together (do not separate)

### Placement
- **Default:** Top right
- **Approved alternate:** Bottom left when top right is visually crowded OR when deity/artwork occupies the upper zone

### Clear Space & Protection
- Keep clear space around logo equal to **at least the height of the inner icon circle**
- **Never:**
  - Tuck into curves
  - Crop it
  - Stretch it
  - Recolour it
  - Place on noisy backgrounds without a clean backing

### Sizing
- **Recommended size:** 7% to 12% of the canvas width
- Logo should be **visible but never larger than** the main headline or hero image

---

## 3. Typography

### Recommended Font Families

**English Headlines**
- Gotham or DinPro
- Should feel bold and direct

**English Body Copy**
- Calibri or Cambria
- Should be simple, readable, and never too compressed

**Hindi & Marathi Headlines**
- Anek Devanagari Series or Tiro Devanagari Hindi

**Hindi & Marathi Supporting Copy**
- Noto Serif Devanagari (when a more traditional tone is needed)

### Typography Rules
- **Use no more than two font families** in a single creative
  - One for headlines
  - One for supporting text
- Headlines should feel **bold and direct**
- Supporting text should be **simple, readable, and never too compressed**

## 4. Colour System: Festival First, Brand Second

### Core Philosophy
Antar Yog should **not force one static palette** on every post/page. Colours should respond to the **festival, deity, ritual, or emotional tone** of the campaign.

However, every design must still carry Antar Yog recognition through:
- **Yellow halo** in the logo
- **Brown logo text**
- **Consistent CTA/footer structure**

## 5. Layout System for All Creatives

### Preferred Formats
- **4:5** — Instagram and Facebook feed
- **9:16** — Stories and vertical formats
- **1:1** — Only when required for platform adaptation

### Consistent Vertical Hierarchy
Use this zone structure for all layouts:

1. **Logo Zone** (top 10–12%)
   - Reserve for logo and optional campaign tag (e.g., "FREE WEBINAR" or "LIVE GLOBAL WEBINAR")

2. **Hero Zone**
   - Use Acharya imagery, deity imagery, or one strong visual idea
   - Do not use too many competing pictures

3. **Headline Zone**
   - Keep one clear promise
   - Large, bold, and instantly readable even on a phone screen

4. **Info Zone**
   - Date, time, duration, venue, Zoom details, dakshina, or seat message
   - Should sit in a structured block

5. **QR / Scan Zone**
   - The scan code should stay in the lower-right area whenever possible
   - Maintain enough clear space to remain scannable

6. **Footer Banner**
   - Compulsory bottom strip
   - Carry the website link and scan code
   - Add phone number or social handles only when format allows without clutter

### Sizing Guidance
- **Hero occupies top 35% to 45%** of the post
- **Registration or CTA strip closes the design at the bottom**

---

## 6. Mandatory Footer Banner

### Required Elements
Every promotional creative should include a fixed footer or banner area containing:
- **Website link** (primary)
- **Scan code / QR code** (scannable, right-aligned)
- Phone number (optional, only if it doesn't shrink the website link or QR code)
- Social handles (optional, can sit below in lighter secondary row)

### Banner Specifications
- Banner can sit across full width or as a clearly visible lower strip
- **Recommended height:** 12% to 16% of total post height
- **Recommended order inside banner:** Website link first, CTA or "register now" second, QR code on the right
- **QR code clearance:** Keep at least 20 px away from outer edge in exported files

## 7. Content & Copy Rules

### Messaging Strategy
- **Lead with one clear spiritual or personal outcome**
- Do not overload a single post with too many benefits
- **3 to 5 benefit points are enough** for most creatives

### Information Hierarchy
- **Date and time should always be easy to find**
- **Registration information should never compete with the headline**

### Text Treatment
- Avoid long paragraphs inside the post
- Use **short lines, icons, separators, and compact information cards**

### Bilingual Communication (English + Hindi/Devanagari)
- Bilingual communication is allowed
- **Hierarchy must stay clear**
- Do not mix English and Devanagari at the same visual level unless intentional

---

## 8. Image Treatment

### Selection Principles
- Use **one primary image per post/section**: 
  - Acharya portrait
  - Deity visual
  - Ritual element
  - Child/student image
  - One symbolic illustration
- Do not use too many competing pictures

### Respect for Sacred Imagery
- Give divine imagery **respect and breathing room**
- **Do not crop sacred visuals awkwardly** through the face or main symbolic element

### Visual Enhancement
- Use **soft glows, radial light, gradients, and festival-specific textures** when they support the mood
- Avoid overly glossy, stock-heavy, or chaotic backgrounds

### Portrait Quality
- Faces should be **bright, clean, and high-resolution**
- If using circular or cut-out portraits:
  - Maintain **consistent edge quality**
  - Maintain **consistent shadow treatment**

---

## 9. Do and Don't Checklist

### ✅ Do
- Keep logo placement consistent (top right by default, bottom left only when needed)
- Let the festival/deity decide the accent palette
- Use one strong headline and one clear CTA
- Keep the QR code functional and large enough to scan
- Maintain a visible footer/banner on every promotional asset

### ❌ Don't
- Randomly change logo colours or build new logo lockups
- Use more than two font families in one post
- Fill every empty area with icons, badges, or bullets
- Hide the website link in tiny type
- Mix unrelated festival colours in one layout
- Crop sacred visuals awkwardly through faces or main symbolic elements

## 10. Production Checklist

### For Event Promotion Posts
Before any creative goes out, verify:

- [ ] Correct logo lockup
- [ ] Correct festival palette
- [ ] Readable headline
- [ ] Visible date and time
- [ ] Working website link
- [ ] Scannable QR code
- [ ] Clean footer banner

### For Campaign Batches
- Keep one master grid and only swap:
  - Palette
  - Headline
  - Imagery
- This makes Antar Yog look more premium and recognizable over time

### For Non-Event Posts
This checklist applies to promotional, informational, devotional, and community-building posts not tied to a specific event.

**Before publishing, verify:**

**1. Branding**
- Logo is present on every post
- Logo is placed in approved position (preferably top right; bottom left as alternate)
- Logo is clearly visible and not competing with background
- Website link and scan code banner is included wherever applicable
- Brand elements are consistent with other Antar Yog creatives

**2. Visual Direction**
- Colours are aligned with the spiritual and festive mood of the post
- Overall visual style feels calm, devotional, premium, and culturally rooted
- Backgrounds, textures, and decorative elements do not overpower the core message
- Imagery is high quality, respectful, and relevant to the topic

**3. Content Accuracy**
- Names, titles, spiritual references, and terminology are correct
- Any quote, teaching, or benefit statement is accurate and approved
- Event photos are relevant and appropriate for public posting
- Spelling, grammar, punctuation, and date references are checked carefully

**4. Layout & Readability**
- Headline is clear and easy to read
- Text is not overcrowded
- Font hierarchy is maintained properly
- Important information is highlighted clearly
- Creative is visually balanced and not cluttered

**5. Purpose of the Post**
- The objective is clear at first glance
- For devotional/teaching posts: takeaway is simple and meaningful
- For promotional posts: CTA is clear without feeling overly commercial
- For photo-based posts: images tell a clear story and feel emotionally aligned with the brand

**6. Platform Suitability**
- Creative dimensions are correct for the platform
- Text size remains legible on mobile
- Key visual and text elements are within safe margins
- Design works well as a standalone post in the feed

**7. Final Review**
- Creative matches Antar Yog's tone and identity
- Nothing feels too loud, commercial, or disconnected from spiritual nature of the brand
- Post has been reviewed for both aesthetics and message clarity
- Final approval has been taken before publishing

---

## Summary of Core Principles

1. **Brand consistency** through logo placement, Acharya imagery, and CTA structure
2. **Festival-responsive design** that adapts colours and tone to context
3. **Clean, premium aesthetic** even when content is devotional or emotional
4. **Clear visual hierarchy** with logo → hero → message → detail → CTA flow
5. **Respect for sacred imagery** with breathing room and high-quality treatment
6. **Simple, focused messaging** with 3–5 benefits maximum
7. **Bilingual readiness** with clear hierarchy between English and Hindi/Devanagari
8. **Mandatory footer/CTA zone** with website link, QR code, and registration info
