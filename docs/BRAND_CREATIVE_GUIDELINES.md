# Antar Yog Brand & Creative Guidelines for Web

**Source:** `Antar_Yog_Creative_Guidelines.pdf`  
**Purpose:** Extract key brand identity, visual language, and design principles applicable to website design and development.

---

## 1. Brand Identity & Tone

Antar Yog should feel:
- **Devotional** — spiritually rooted and reverent
- **Credible** — organised, authoritative, trustworthy
- **Transformational** — aspiring, hopeful, outcome-focused
- **Premium** — clean, well-spaced, thoughtfully composed
- **Cultural** — respectful of Indian spiritual traditions

### Three Visual Anchors (Always Present)
1. **Identity Mark (Logo)** — mandatory on every page/section
2. **Acharya Imagery** — portrait of Guru Acharyaji (respect and credibility)
3. **Strong CTA Zone** — prominent call-to-action placement at bottom of sections

---

## 2. Logo System

### Placement
- **Default:** Top right of page/section
- **Alternate:** Bottom left when top-right is visually crowded or when deity/imagery occupies upper zone

### Sizing & Spacing
- **Size range:** 7–12% of canvas width (desktop), scale responsively for mobile
- **Clearance:** Minimum clear space around logo = height of the inner icon circle
- **Never:** Recolour, stretch, crop, tuck into curves, or place on noisy backgrounds without clean backing

### Rules
- Use only approved Antar Yog logo lockups (English and Devanagari versions with symbol + wordmark)
- Logo should be visible but never larger than main headline or hero image
- Maintain consistent placement across all pages for brand recognition

---

## 3. Typography

### Font Families (as per brand guidelines)

**English**
- Headlines: Gotham or DinPro (bold, direct feel)
- Body copy: Calibri or Cambria (readable, clean)
- *Note: Website currently uses Lora (serif) for headings and Inter (sans) for body — these are compatible alternatives in the same spirit*

**Hindi & Marathi**
- Headlines: Anek Devanagari Series or Tiro Devanagari Hindi
- Body/supporting copy: Noto Serif Devanagari (when a traditional tone is needed)

### Usage Rules
- **Max two font families per layout** — one for headlines, one for body text
- Headlines should feel **bold and direct**
- Supporting text should be **simple, readable, and never too compressed**
- Bilingual layouts: Keep hierarchy clear; do not mix English and Devanagari at the same visual level unless intentional

## 4. Colour System: Festival First, Brand Second

Antar Yog should not force one static palette on every page. Colours should respond to the festival, deity, ritual, or emotional tone of the section or campaign.

**Brand Consistency Rule:** Every design carries Antar Yog recognition through:
- Yellow halo in logo
- Brown logo text
- Consistent CTA/footer structure

## 5. Layout Hierarchy

Use a consistent vertical flow for all pages and major sections:

1. **Logo Zone** — Top 10–12% (logo + optional campaign tag like "FREE WEBINAR")
2. **Hero Zone** — Acharya imagery, deity visual, or one strong symbolic visual
3. **Message Zone** — Clear headline with one strong promise
4. **Detail Zone** — Structured info blocks (dates, times, benefits, etc.)
5. **CTA / Banner Zone** — Bottom 12–16% (registration, website link, QR code)

### Sizing Guidance
- Hero occupies **top 35–45%** of visual area
- CTA/registration strip **closes the design at the bottom**
- Ensures upward flow and clear visual hierarchy

---

## 6. Image Treatment

### Selection
- Use **one primary image per section**: Acharya portrait, deity visual, ritual element, child/student image, or symbolic illustration
- Avoid too many competing pictures in a single layout

### Respect for Sacred Imagery
- Give divine imagery **breathing room and respect**
- Do not crop sacred visuals awkwardly through faces or main symbolic elements
- High-resolution, bright, clean portraiture

### Visual Techniques
- Use **soft glows, radial light, gradients** and festival-specific textures when they support the mood
- Avoid overly glossy, stock-heavy, or chaotic backgrounds
- For circular or cut-out portraits: maintain **consistent edge quality and shadow treatment**

---

## 7. Content & Copy Rules

### Messaging
- **Lead with one clear spiritual or personal outcome** per section
- Do not overload with too many benefits — **3–5 benefit points maximum**
- Date and time should always be easy to find; registration info should never compete with headline

### Text Treatment
- Avoid long paragraphs inside sections
- Use **short lines, icons, separators, and compact information cards**
- Keep text scannable and mobile-friendly

### Bilingual Communication (English + Hindi/Devanagari)
- Bilingual layouts are allowed and encouraged
- **Hierarchy must stay clear** — do not mix English and Devanagari at the same visual level unless intentional
- Use visual weight (font size, colour) to establish primary/secondary relationship

---

## 8. CTA & Footer Banner Structure

Every promotional or event section should include a **fixed footer or banner area**.

### Banner Contents (in order of importance)
1. **Website link** (primary)
2. **CTA or "Register Now"** button (secondary)
3. **QR code** (right side)
4. **Phone number** (optional, only if space allows)
5. **Social handles** (optional secondary row, lighter treatment)

### Sizing & Spacing
- **Recommended height:** 12–16% of total section height
- **QR code clearance:** At least 20px from outer edge
- **Scannable space:** QR code must remain large and unobstructed

---

## 9. Design Principles: Do & Don't

### ✅ Do
- Keep logo placement consistent (top right by default, bottom left only when needed)
- Let the festival/deity context decide the accent palette
- Use one strong headline and one clear CTA
- Keep QR code functional and large enough to scan
- Maintain a visible footer/banner on every promotional asset
- Use Acharya imagery and high-quality portraiture
- Give sacred visuals breathing room

### ❌ Don't
- Randomly change logo colours or build new logo lockups
- Use more than two font families in one section
- Fill every empty area with icons, badges, or bullets (risk of visual clutter)
- Hide the website link in tiny type
- Mix unrelated festival colours in one layout
- Crop sacred imagery awkwardly through faces
- Place logos on noisy backgrounds without clean backing

---

## 10. Web Implementation Checklist

Before launching any page section, verify:

### Branding
- ✓ Logo is present and in approved position
- ✓ Logo is visible and not competing with background
- ✓ Footer banner with website link and QR code is included
- ✓ Brand elements consistent with other Antar Yog pages

### Visual Direction
- ✓ Colours aligned with spiritual and festive mood
- ✓ Overall style feels calm, devotional, premium, culturally rooted
- ✓ Backgrounds and textures do not overpower the core message
- ✓ Imagery is high quality, respectful, and relevant

### Content & Layout
- ✓ Headline is clear and easy to read (especially on mobile)
- ✓ Text is not overcrowded; hierarchy is maintained
- ✓ Important information is highlighted clearly
- ✓ Creative is visually balanced, not cluttered

### Copy & Accuracy
- ✓ Names, titles, spiritual references are accurate
- ✓ Any quote or teaching attributed to Guru Acharyaji is approved
- ✓ Spelling, grammar, and dates are checked
- ✓ CTA is clear without feeling overly commercial

### Platform Readiness
- ✓ Dimensions correct for intended platform/device
- ✓ Text remains legible on mobile
- ✓ Key elements within safe margins
- ✓ Tone and identity feel authentic and aligned
